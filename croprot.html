Why Rotate Crops?

Crop rotation is essential for maintaining soil health and improving agricultural productivity. By alternating crops with different nutrient requirements, crop rotation prevents soil depletion and minimizes pest and disease buildup associated with monoculture. It helps replenish soil nutrients naturally, reducing the need for chemical fertilizers, and promotes biodiversity by supporting a range of microorganisms beneficial to soil structure and fertility. Additionally, crop rotation enhances soil moisture retention and reduces erosion, making it a sustainable practice that contributes to long-term soil conservation and agricultural resilience.

Soil Health

Different crops have different nutrient needs and contribute different benefits to soil health.

Pest Management

Rotating crops helps break pest and disease cycles that occur when one type of crop is grown in the same location year after year.
